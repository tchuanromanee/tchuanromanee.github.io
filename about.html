I’m Tee, a third year PhD student working in the HCI Lab at Notre Dame under Dr. Ron Metoyer.

My pronouns are they/them.

Background

I’m born and raised in Ann Arbor. I went to Milwaukee School of Engineering for one year in undergrad, then Kettering University for the other 3 years. While attending Kettering University, I worked for Bosch for four three-month terms. My previous areas of work include embedded systems, website development and administration, computer vision and image analysis, machine learning, and accounting and bookkeeping.

I like to tinker with website and content management (eg. Drupal, Django, JavaScript, VueJS, etc), managing websites for multiple organizations.

My Research

My areas of interest lie in the applications of computer science to healthcare. I'm interested in how technology can better serve the trans community by making healthcare and transition more accessible.

I'm also interested in designing and improving solutions for technologically mediated mental health treatment.

Projects

I've been involved in multiple computing projects outside of research.

Diagnostic Assistance Software for Mental Health Care Providers

Undergraduate thesis project to assist mental health care providers by developing a neural network that predicts possible diagnoses of mental disorders. Developed using Theano running on top of Keras.

Kettering University Open Forum

Campus-wide online forum for students to discuss issues of day-to-day life. Developed in conjunction with Cole Heathcock, Tyler Craig, George Reeves, and Andrew Cicchinelli as part of Web Software Tools term project using HTML/CSS, PHP, and MySQL.
